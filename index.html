Birth and Early Life

First known photo of Billie Jean King, c. 1944

Billie Jean King, born on the 22nd of November, 1943, was a remarkable figure in both sports and aviation. Her parents were a shoemaker and a firefighter. Growing up in Long Beach, California, she showed an early interest in tennis and airplanes, and would go on to break both sports and aviation records.

Tennis Career

In 1945 Billie Jean King played her first game of tennis at the age of 2. At the age of 8, Billie Jean King won her first tennis tournament, the "Little League of Champions," showcasing her natural talent for the sport. She trained rigorously at a local tennis club and quickly rose through the ranks, becoming the youngest player to win the Wimbledon Championships in 1955!

The "Battle of the Sexes" was a famous tennis match between 29-year-old Billie Jean King and 55-year-old Bobby Riggs. King impressed the world when she won against the previously undefeated Riggs, proving that women can play tennis as well as men.

Accomplishments as an Aviator

Aviation and Tennis Trophies won in 1995

While Billie Jean King is more widely known for her accomplishments in tennis, she is also an innovator in aviation. She obtained her pilot's license at the age of 16 and flew her own plane to tournaments across the country. Her unique approach to combining tennis and aviation made her a beloved figure among fans and pilots alike. In 1967, Billie Jean King made history by becoming the first person to play tennis in zero gravity during a special exhibition match aboard a NASA spacecraft. Although the match ended in a tie due to the floating tennis ball, it captured the imagination of people worldwide.
